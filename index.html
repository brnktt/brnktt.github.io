About

I spent seven years as a .NET / C# backend engineer inside financial infrastructure: live payment processing for cash-terminal networks, market-data pipelines for an investment fund platform, observability for microservice fleets. Along the way I caught fraudulent trade anomalies with statistical methods and kept payment systems running with zero critical incidents.

Since 2024 I've been trading proprietary capital, and the two halves finally clicked: engineering discipline and market experience rarely live in the same person. I'm building a career at that intersection, with quantitative research and trading as the long-term target.

When the markets are closed you'll find me deep in Python and C++, working through the math that quant desks actually use.
